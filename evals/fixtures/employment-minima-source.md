# STATEMENT OF TERMS AND CONDITIONS OF EMPLOYMENT

**THIS AGREEMENT** is made on **27/03/2023**

**BETWEEN:**

1. **HARROWGATE FULFILMENT SERVICES LIMITED**, a company registered in England and Wales, whose registered office is at Unit 14, Calder Vale Trading Estate, Ossett Road, Wakefield WF5 8QT (the "**Company**"); and

2. **DANIEL CHUKWUEMEKA OKAFOR** of 62 Ravensthorpe Grove, Leeds LS11 6EP (the "**Employee**").

---

## 1. Commencement and continuous employment

1.1 The Employee's employment with the Company will commence on **03/04/2023**.

1.2 No employment with a previous employer counts towards the Employee's period of continuous employment with the Company.

## 2. Job title and duties

2.1 The Employee is employed as a **Warehouse Team Leader**.

2.2 The Employee will perform such duties as are consistent with the role, together with any other reasonable duties the Company may require from time to time.

## 3. Place of work

3.1 The Employee's normal place of work is Unit 14, Calder Vale Trading Estate, Ossett Road, Wakefield WF5 8QT. The Company may require the Employee to work at any of its other sites within a 25-mile radius of Wakefield on reasonable notice. The Employee will not be required to work outside the United Kingdom.

## 4. Remuneration

4.1 The Employee's salary is **£24,000 per annum**, payable monthly in arrears on or around the 28th day of each month by credit transfer to the Employee's nominated bank account.

4.2 The Company may deduct from the Employee's salary any sums owed by the Employee to the Company.

## 5. Hours of work

5.1 The Employee's normal hours of work are **45 hours per week**, worked in shifts of nine hours (including a 30-minute unpaid break) between 06:00 and 22:00, Monday to Saturday, in accordance with the rota published by the Company.

5.2 The Employee may be required to work such additional hours as are reasonably necessary for the proper performance of the Employee's duties. No overtime payment will be made unless authorised in advance in writing by the Operations Director.

## 6. Working time

6.1 **By signing this Agreement the Employee agrees that the limit on average weekly working time set out in the Working Time Regulations 1998 shall not apply to the Employee's employment.** This clause is a condition of the Employee's employment with the Company.

## 7. Holidays

7.1 The Company's holiday year runs from 1 January to 31 December.

7.2 The Employee is entitled to **20 days' paid holiday in each holiday year, inclusive of all bank and public holidays**. The Employee may be required to work on bank and public holidays in accordance with the rota, in which case a day in lieu will be deducted from the 20-day entitlement rather than added to it.

7.3 Holiday must be taken at times convenient to the Company and approved in advance by the Employee's line manager. Untaken holiday may not be carried forward into the next holiday year and no payment will be made in lieu of untaken holiday except on termination.

## 8. Sickness absence

8.1 The Employee must notify the Employee's line manager of any sickness absence by 05:30 on the first day of absence.

8.2 **The Company does not operate any sick pay scheme and no payment will be made by the Company in respect of any period of absence due to sickness or injury.**

## 9. Notice of termination

9.1 The employment may be terminated by **either party giving to the other not less than one week's notice in writing**.

9.2 The Company may terminate the employment without notice or payment in lieu of notice in the event of gross misconduct.

9.3 The Company reserves the right to make a payment in lieu of notice.

## 10. Collective agreements

10.1 There are no collective agreements which directly affect the terms and conditions of the Employee's employment.

## 11. Disciplinary and grievance procedures

11.1 The Company's disciplinary rules and disciplinary and grievance procedures are set out in the Staff Handbook, a copy of which is available from the site office. These procedures do not form part of the Employee's contract of employment.

11.2 If the Employee wishes to raise a grievance, or to appeal a disciplinary decision, the Employee should apply in writing to the Operations Director.

## 12. Probationary period

12.1 The first three months of the Employee's employment will be a probationary period, during which the Company will assess the Employee's suitability for the role. The Company may extend the probationary period by up to a further three months.

## 13. Entire agreement

13.1 This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions, arrangements and understandings relating to the Employee's employment.
