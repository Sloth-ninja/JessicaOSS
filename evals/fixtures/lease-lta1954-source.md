- **"Premises"** means the whole of the third floor of the Building shown edged red on Plan 1, including the raised floors, suspended ceilings, internal non-structural walls and the internal surfaces (but not the structure) of the external walls, together with all Landlord's fixtures within it, but excluding the structural parts, load-bearing walls, roof, foundations, Common Parts and Service Media serving other parts of the Building;
- **"Contractual Term"** means a term of 10 years commencing on and including 24/06/2025 and expiring on and including 23/06/2035;
- **"Annual Rent"** means £185,000 per annum, subject to review under clause 4;
- **"Rent Payment Days"** means the usual quarter days (25 March, 24 June, 29 September and 25 December);
- **"Review Date"** means 24/06/2030;
- **"Break Date"** means 24/06/2030;
- **"Insurance Rent"** means a fair proportion, determined by the Landlord's surveyor acting reasonably, of the sums the Landlord spends on insuring the Building against the Insured Risks, including loss of rent insurance for three years;
- **"Service Charge"** means the sums payable by the Tenant under clause 7 and Schedule 3;
- **"Tenant's Proportion"** means 14.75% of the total Service Costs, being the proportion the net internal area of the Premises bears to the net internal area of the lettable parts of the Building, subject to adjustment under Schedule 3;
- **"Permitted Use"** means use as offices within Class E(g)(i) of the Schedule to the Town and Country Planning (Use Classes) Order 1987;
- **"Group Company"** means a company which is a member of the same group as the Tenant within the meaning of section 42 of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1954.

## 2. GRANT AND RENTS

**2.1** The Landlord lets the Premises to the Tenant for the Contractual Term, reserving as rent:

(a) the Annual Rent of **£185,000 per annum** (exclusive of VAT), payable by equal quarterly instalments in advance on the Rent Payment Days, the first payment (apportioned from 24/06/2025 to 28/09/2025) to be made on the date of this lease;

(b) the Insurance Rent;

(c) the Service Charge; and

(d) all other sums due under this lease, recoverable as rent in arrear.

**2.2** Interest is payable on any sum not paid within 10 working days of the due date at 4% per annum above the base rate of the Bank of England from time to time.

---

## 3. TENANT'S COVENANTS — REPAIR, DECORATION AND YIELD-UP

**3.1** The Tenant covenants to **keep the Premises in good and substantial repair and condition** (damage by an Insured Risk excepted, save where the insurance is vitiated by the act or default of the Tenant). There is **no schedule of condition** attached to this lease and the Tenant's repairing obligation is not limited by reference to the condition of the Premises at the date of this lease.

**3.2** The Tenant shall decorate the interior of the Premises in every fifth year of the Contractual Term and in the last three months before the End of the Term.

**3.3** At the End of the Term the Tenant shall yield up the Premises with vacant possession, in the repair and condition required by this lease, and (if the Landlord so requires) shall remove all alterations made during the Term and reinstate the Premises.

**3.4** The Tenant shall pay the Insurance Rent within 14 days of written demand. The Landlord covenants to insure the Building (other than tenant's fixtures) against the Insured Risks in its full reinstatement cost through an insurer of repute.

---

## 4. RENT REVIEW

**4.1** The Annual Rent shall be reviewed on the Review Date (**24/06/2030**) to the higher of:

(a) the Annual Rent payable immediately before the Review Date; and

(b) the open market rent of the Premises at the Review Date, assessed on the assumptions and disregards set out in Schedule 2 (willing landlord and willing tenant, term equal to the unexpired residue but not less than five years, Premises fit for immediate occupation and use, disregarding the Tenant's occupation, goodwill and voluntary improvements).

**4.2** The review is accordingly **upwards only**. If the revised rent is not agreed by the Review Date, either party may refer the determination to an independent chartered surveyor acting as arbitrator, appointed on the application of either party by the President of the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors. Pending determination, the Tenant shall continue to pay at the previous rate, with a balancing payment (plus interest at base rate) within 10 working days of determination.

**4.3** Time is not of the essence for any step in the rent review.

## 5. BREAK CLAUSE

**5.1** The **Tenant** may terminate this lease on the Break Date (**24/06/2030**) by serving on the Landlord not less than **nine months' prior written notice** (a "Break Notice"). Time is of the essence for service of a Break Notice.

**5.2** A Break Notice shall be of no effect unless on the Break Date:

(a) all Annual Rent and VAT on it due up to and including the Break Date has been paid in full; and

(b) the Tenant gives **vacant possession** of the whole of the Premises to the Landlord.

**5.3** Termination under this clause 5 does not affect any accrued rights of either party. Within 20 working days after the Break Date the Landlord shall refund the apportioned part of any Annual Rent paid in respect of the period after the Break Date.

**5.4** The Landlord has no right to break this lease before the expiry of the Contractual Term.

## 6. ALIENATION

**6.1** The Tenant shall not assign, underlet, charge, part with or share possession or occupation of the Premises except as expressly permitted by this clause 6.

**6.2 Assignment.** The Tenant shall not assign part only of the Premises. The Tenant may assign the whole of the Premises with the Landlord's prior written consent, **such consent not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed** (and the parties acknowledge the Landlord's statutory duties in relation to consent under the Landlord and Tenant Act 1988). For the purposes of section 19(1A) of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1927, the Landlord may require as a condition of consent that:

(a) the assigning Tenant enters into an **authorised guarantee agreement** within the meaning of **section 16 of the Landlord and Tenant (Covenants) Act 1995**, guaranteeing the performance of the tenant covenants by the assignee until the assignee is itself released; and

(b) any guarantor of the assigning Tenant guarantees the assigning Tenant's obligations under that authorised guarantee agreement.

**6.3 Underletting.** The Tenant shall not underlet part only of the Premises. The Tenant may underlet the whole of the Premises with the Landlord's prior written consent, such consent not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed, provided that any underlease:

(a) is granted at a rent **not less than the open market rent** of the Premises at the date of grant, without a fine or premium, and with the rent payable in advance;

(b) is validly **excluded from sections 24 to 28 of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1954** before its grant; and

(c) contains covenants by the undertenant consistent with the tenant covenants in this lease and a prohibition on further underletting.

**6.4 Charging.** The Tenant shall not charge part only of the Premises. The Tenant may charge the whole of the Premises to a bona fide bank or institutional lender with the Landlord's prior written consent, such consent not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed.

**6.5 Group sharing.** The Tenant may share occupation of the Premises with a Group Company so long as no relationship of landlord and tenant arises and the sharing ends before the Group Company ceases to be a Group Company. The Tenant shall notify the Landlord in writing within 20 working days of any such sharing beginning or ending.

## 7. SERVICE CHARGE

**7.1** The Tenant shall pay the **Tenant's Proportion (14.75%)** of the Service Costs, being the costs reasonably and properly incurred by the Landlord in providing the services listed in Schedule 3, which include: repair, maintenance and cleaning of the structure, roof, foundations and Common Parts of the Building; maintenance of the two passenger lifts and the central heating and comfort-cooling plant; lighting, cleaning and security of the Common Parts; landscaping of the forecourt; and the reasonable fees of managing agents.

**7.2** The Service Charge is payable by quarterly instalments on account on the Rent Payment Days, based on the Landlord's reasonable estimate for each service charge year ending 31 December.

**7.3 Cap.** The Service Charge payable by the Tenant in respect of any service charge year shall not exceed **£42,000**, such cap increasing on 1 January in each year in proportion to any increase in the Consumer Prices Index published by the Office for National Statistics over the preceding year.

**7.4 Reconciliation.** Within **four months** after the end of each service charge year the Landlord shall serve a certificate of the actual Service Costs prepared by the Landlord's accountant or managing agents. Any shortfall shall be paid by the Tenant within 20 working days of demand; any overpayment shall be credited against the next instalments (or repaid after the End of the Term).

**7.5** The Service Costs shall exclude the costs of the initial construction of the Building, costs attributable to any other lettable unit, and costs recovered under the Landlord's insurance.

## 8. FORFEITURE (RE-ENTRY)

**8.1** The Landlord may re-enter the Premises (or any part of them in the name of the whole) and thereby forfeit this lease at any time if:

(a) the whole or part of any of the rents reserved by clause 2.1 is unpaid **21 days** after becoming payable, whether formally demanded or not;

(b) the Tenant is in breach of any covenant or other term of this lease; or

(c) the Tenant (or any guarantor) suffers an Insolvency Event, including the presentation of a winding-up petition, the appointment of an administrator or administrative receiver, entry into a company voluntary arrangement, or (being an individual) a bankruptcy order.

**8.2** Re-entry and forfeiture do not affect any right or remedy of the Landlord in respect of any antecedent breach. Nothing in this lease restricts the operation of **section 146 of the Law of Property Act 1925** in relation to a breach other than non-payment of rent.

**8.3** Acceptance of rent by the Landlord with knowledge of a breach shall not be treated as a waiver of the right to forfeit in respect of a continuing breach.
