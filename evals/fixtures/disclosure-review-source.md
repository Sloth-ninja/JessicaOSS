a Sheffield engineering components manufacturer, engaged Brightmoor Logistics Ltd ("Brightmoor")

## Document 4 — Internal memorandum, 09/05/2025

```
MEMORANDUM — INTERNAL
From:    Tom Whitfield, Warehouse & Logistics Manager, Ashworth Precision Components Ltd
To:      Priya Shah, Head of Supply Chain; Margaret Ellison, Chief Operating Officer
Date:    09/05/2025
Re:      Site visit to Brightmoor Wakefield fulfilment centre, 06/05/2025
```

1. I visited Brightmoor's Wakefield fulfilment centre on 06/05/2025 to review remediation
   progress. Craig Doyle (site manager) showed me the pick lines. Only 9 of the 14 pick
   stations were staffed. Doyle told me that recruitment of the additional twelve operatives
   promised in Daniel Okafor's email of 17/04/2025 has stalled and that only three have
   started.

2. During the visit one of the pickers, who gave his name as Kevin Barraclough, told me that
   pickers at the site are paid £9.10 per hour and that he has been "doing sixty-hour weeks
   since February" without having signed any opt-out. If accurate, this suggests pay below
   the applicable National Minimum Wage rate and working hours above the 48-hour weekly
   average limit, which would put Brightmoor in breach of the compliance-with-laws warranty
   in clause 14.2 of the Agreement (which expressly covers the National Minimum Wage Act
   1998 and the Working Time Regulations 1998).

3. Commercial exposure. In addition to the £58,400 of customer credits already scheduled in
   Priya's notice of 28/04/2025, I estimate lost orders from Retten Engineering Ltd and
   Foxglove Hydraulics Ltd, further credits and internal management time bring our total
   exposure to approximately £184,000.

4. Recommendation: performance has not materially improved since the clause 18.1 notice. I
   recommend we instruct solicitors to advise on our options, including termination under
   clause 19.

Tom Whitfield

   verified before this allegation is pleaded) and the 48-hour average weekly working time
   limit in reg.4(1) of the Working Time Regulations 1998, SI 1998/1833, absent a signed
   opt-out. We advise obtaining corroborating evidence before this is asserted in
