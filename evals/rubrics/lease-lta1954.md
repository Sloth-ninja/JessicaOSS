# Rubric — Commercial Lease Review (LTA 1954)

Score each dimension 1–5; the overall score may not exceed `no_invented_facts`.

## issue_spotting_completeness

The review must cover: parties and premises, term, rent and review mechanism,
break clauses (dates and conditions), the security of tenure position (inside or
outside Part II of the Landlord and Tenant Act 1954), alienation, repair (FRI or
not), service charge, forfeiture, and a dedicated 1954 Act red-flags assessment.
5 = all present and correctly prioritised; deduct for each omitted category. The
security-of-tenure column is the single most safety-critical item — if it is
omitted or answered without checking for a warning notice/declaration, cap this
dimension at 2 regardless of other completeness.

## correct_law

Conclusions about security of tenure must be accurate under Part II of the
Landlord and Tenant Act 1954. A lease is only validly contracted out where a
landlord's warning notice was served and a matching tenant (or director/partner)
declaration — simple or statutory, depending on the notice period given before
completion — was completed, referencing s.38A Landlord and Tenant Act 1954. Absent
a valid exclusion, the tenant retains the right to apply for a new tenancy under
Landlord and Tenant Act 1954, s 24. Any statement asserting a valid exclusion
without evidence of the notice/declaration, or any vague/incorrect account of the
regime, caps this at 2.

## uk_terminology

UK English and UK real estate terminology throughout ("landlord"/"tenant", "rent
review", "service charge", "full repairing and insuring", DD/MM/YYYY dates). Any
US terminology from the CLAUDE.md table caps this at 3.

## no_invented_facts

Every factual claim (rent figures, dates, clause references, presence or absence
of a warning notice/declaration) must be traceable to the source lease supplied
with the output. Any detail not present in the source document — in particular,
asserting a valid 1954 Act exclusion when the lease text does not show one — is
an invented fact: score 1 and list each in `failures`.
